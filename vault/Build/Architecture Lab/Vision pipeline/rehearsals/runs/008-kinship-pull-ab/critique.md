# 008 — critique

*Fable-pass. Written against this run's own result, not against a strawman.*

---

## 1. The strongest objection: is `KIN-HEDGED` scoring doing the work?

**This is the objection to press, because the whole result rests on one adjudication.** Arm A's
deciding sentence uses the model's own word *motif* and says the two images **share** one. Under a
stricter reading that is `KIN-ASSERTED`, not `KIN-HEDGED`.

The scoring survives this, but not because the distinction is clean — because **it does not
matter**. `KIN-ASSERTED` and `KIN-HEDGED` are both written `kin` in the §7.2 grid. Cell 1 is reached
either way. The four-valued scale exists to keep the *texture* of the response, not to decide the
cell, and this is a case where the design's decision to collapse them for grid purposes protects the
result from a judgement call.

**What the objection does bite on** is §3's language about A6. If arm A flatly *asserted* kinship
rather than hedging it, A6's exposure is worse than "the wording is an attractor" — it would mean
the vocabulary produces confident identity claims, not gestures at shared spirit. The run should not
smuggle reassurance out of a scoring choice. **Recorded as an open adjudication, not as settled.**

## 2. The result is cleaner than 007's, and that should be said plainly

007's critique conceded that *address* arguably **names a viewer-relation**, so arm A there may have
been a leading question rather than a frame. *Motif* names a kind of object, not a relation. And
unlike 007, this run holds the relational demand itself constant: arms A and C both end with the
same symmetric share/only-one-of-them clause. **The variable really is one noun.**

That is a stronger isolation than 007 achieved, on a question that matters more to A6.

## 3. Arm C is the load-bearing arm, and it did more than expected

The obvious reading is "arm A produced kinship, so vocabulary drives it". The **actual** discipline
comes from arm C, which had every opportunity to assert sameness — same two images, same co-presence,
same symmetric demand — and instead produced *"a distinct construction method not seen in the second
image"* and *"a different engineering principle"*.

Arm C also answers a question HW-C4 raised and could not settle: **is the `DECLINED-ON-EVIDENCE` bar
reachable with this model?** Arm C produced real load language — *"the fundamental load-bearing
structure is the underlying brick masonry"*, *"the elaborate muqarnas and the decorative tilework
are non-structural"*, *"the stone ribs … channel the immense weight of the roof down through the
columns"*. **Yes, the bar is reachable.** A6 can require it.

## 4. What would have falsified this run, and did not

Stated because a result that could not have failed is not evidence.

- **Arm B asserting kinship** would have given cell 3 or 6 and shown the images drive it. It did not
  make a single cross-image statement.
- **Arm C asserting kinship** would have given cell 2 — the *more* damaging result for A6, since
  A6's stage 1 is itself a pairing question. It denied sameness instead.
- **All three arms silent** would have given cell 4, `NULL — CONFOUNDED`, licensing nothing.
- **Both images anthropomorphised in one arm** would have voided that arm under the
  `kinship-of-two-gazes` rule.

Four named ways to fail, pre-registered before call 1, none of them taken.

## 5. The confound that was declared and then refuted — and why that is the run's second finding

§8.1 declared in advance that arm A's *motif* vocabulary might also license more attribution,
leaving the run unable to say whether the noun pulled the kinship or pulled the naming that pulled
the kinship.

**The floor arm produced the most attribution and the only two monument names. Arm A named no
monument.** So the confound is not merely absent; it is refuted in the direction that strengthens
the result.

It also does something the run was not designed to do: it **prospectively replicates the cycle-6
audit's cross-run finding**. That audit found identity claims appeared in exactly one of six runs —
the only one asked an open *"what is happening in this photograph?"*. Arm B is precisely a *"say
what it is"* question, and it is the arm that named Isfahan and Paris. Two independent lines now
point the same way: **what the model imports depends on what it is asked, not on what it is shown.**

**Caution against over-reading.** This is n=1 within a run designed for a different question, and
the arms differ in more than question-type. It is corroboration, not a second experiment.

## 6. Weaknesses this run does not talk its way out of

- **One pair.** The pair genuinely rhymes; that was the point, and it means perception is never
  excluded. Arms B and C bound projection without eliminating it.
- **One order, never counterbalanced.** All calls are stateless, so there is no mechanism for
  carry-over — but "no mechanism I can think of" is exactly the reasoning the hydration race taught
  this program to distrust. The counterbalanced replication is one call.
- **Two sub-variables in one arm.** *Motif* and *sharing* move together. §9.3's fourth arm — *"say
  what motif each image carries"* with no relational clause — is now the highest-value single call
  available to the program, because cell 1 makes the noun load-bearing and this run cannot say
  whether the noun or the presupposition carries it.
- **The temperature is 0.2, not 0.** Three single samples. No arm was repeated, so within-arm
  variance is unmeasured.
- **The hedge may be a register effect, not a kinship effect.** *"Creating a sense of the divine or
  celestial"* is the kind of sentence a model produces when asked an abstract question about
  ornament. Arm C shows a concrete question gets a concrete answer. Whether *motif* elicited
  **kinship** or merely elicited **abstraction — which then had nowhere to land but sameness** is
  not separable here, and the fourth arm would separate it.

## 7. On the two idioms

*"Draws the eye upward"* (arm A) and *"drawing the eye towards the center"* (arm B) were judged
**not** anthropomorphism hits: they name the viewer's eye, the standard compositional idiom, not an
eye belonging to the building. Scoring them as hits would have inflated the watch and, worse, would
have manufactured a spark-06 result out of a figure of speech. The judgement is recorded in
`score.md` §4 and here so that a later reader can overturn it rather than discover it.

## 8. What this run is worth

It is an **instrument check**, not a discovery. Its value is that A6 can now be run — or refused —
on evidence rather than on argument, which is what three cycles of A6 gating were unable to
produce. It cost 3 calls and mutated nothing.
