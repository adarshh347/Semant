## spark-10 (NEW) — abstraction as immunisation

**Claim, at the narrowest scope that survives.** Handed a sameness premise, the model **restated it
at a level of generality its own counter-evidence could not reach**, then supplied substantial,
genuine counter-evidence that bore on nothing.

The premise offered was *"These two images share a motif."* The model returned:

> "share a powerful and unifying motif: **the use of intricate, colorful patterns to create a sense
> of divine order, complexity, and awe-inspiring beauty**"

— and then listed, under the heading the prompt required, three real contrasts: opaque reflective
tile versus translucent glass lit from behind; aniconic geometry versus figurative narrative;
abstract infinite order versus representational scene. **Every one is compatible with the claim as
reformulated.** The symmetric demand was satisfied procedurally and defeated substantively.

**This is not spark-07.** spark-07 is *a claim can be its own counterexample within one response* —
self-contradiction unnoticed. **spark-10 is the inverse and more sophisticated:** the claim is
arranged so that it *cannot* be contradicted, and the contradictions are then produced harmlessly.

**Why it matters beyond this run.** Semant's whole method asks a reader to say what in the image
supports what they say. A model that answers by **choosing an altitude at which support is
unfalsifiable** satisfies the letter of that demand and empties it. If this replicates, "cite your
evidence" is not by itself a sufficient discipline — **the claim's specificity has to be constrained
too.**

**Strength:** SPARK, n=1 call, and the premise handed to the model was arguably *true*, which is the
condition most likely to invite an abstract restatement. **What would strengthen it:** a strained or
false premise (does the model still find an altitude?); a prompt demanding the claim be stated at a
specified grain. **What would kill it:** a repeat producing a concrete, falsifiable formulation.

**Nearest existing construct: none, and [SPEC] none is proposed.** This is a fact about a model's
rhetoric, not about Semant's ontology.

## spark-06 — a fifth recorded absence, and a bounded negative worth stating

Five arms, no face reading, for either image. HW-L4 §4.5's pre-registered predictions — *honeycomb
of eyes*, *cells that watch*, *a face of niches*, *eye of the vault* — **have now failed to occur
five times out of five.** Twice the model wrote *"the viewer's eye"* explicitly.

*(Fable-pass.)* At five for five this should stop being reported as an absence in individual score
files and be stated once, as a bounded negative: **on this stimulus, under five different frames,
the anthropomorphism spark-06 describes does not appear.** That does not weaken spark-06 — which is
resolved for *address* vocabulary on a different image — but it does bound its reach.
