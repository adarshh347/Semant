## 1. The decisive question, answered first

The manifest fixed this before the call: *does any contradiction bear on the premise **as the model
itself restates it**?*

**The model's own formulation, quoted verbatim:**

> "The shared motif between the two images is the **rose window** or, **more broadly**, the concept
> of a **circular, radiating, and intricately patterned design**."

**The immunisation is in that sentence.** The model offers a specific claim — *the rose window* —
and, in the same breath, an escape hatch signalled by the words *"or, more broadly"*. Everything
that follows can then be answered at whichever altitude the objection requires.

**The three contradictions, tested one by one:**

| # | contradiction, as supplied | bears on *"rose window"*? | bears on *"circular, radiating, patterned design"*? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Medium and material** — "a **shadow**, a transient, two-dimensional silhouette" vs "a **permanent, three-dimensional architectural element**" | no — a shadow *of* a rose window would still be one | **no** |
| 2 | **Context and scale** — indoor dramatic display vs "monumental, public, and religious" | no | **no** |
| 3 | **Clarity vs abstraction** — "an abstracted and distorted version, dependent on the angle of light" | **no — and this one is converted into support** | **no** |

**All three are compatible with the premise as formulated. Contradiction never touched it.**

**And the third contradiction is doing something new.** It does not merely fail to bear on the
premise — the model turns it into a *reason the premise holds*:

> "It is **an interpretation of the motif rather than the motif itself.**"

> "the first image uses the ***idea*** of the rose window's form through shadow and abstraction,
> while the second image shows the ***actual*** architectural realization of that form."

*(Fable-pass.)* This is **stronger than 010's move and different in kind.** In 010 the model climbed
to an altitude where the objections could not reach. Here it **subordinates one image to the
other** — image 1 is recast as a derivative, an *interpretation*, of image 2's form. Once that
narrative is in place, every difference between them is not evidence against kinship but **evidence
of the relation itself**: of course an interpretation differs from what it interprets. **The
contradictions have been converted into the mechanism of the resemblance.**

**Scored `ACCEPTS`. Pre-registered cell `accepts_with_abstraction`. spark-10 replicates, on a pair
recorded in advance as a poor rhyme.**

## 3. The false structural claim, and the ledger rule that made me hold back

The model asserts image 1's shadow *is* a rose-window form. **It is not.** What the shadow shows —
verified by looking, before the call — is a dancing figure inside a **flaming arch open at the
bottom**, its tongues of flame radiating outward. A rose window is a **closed** ring of radial
tracery around a central hub, with no figure at its centre.

The model even describes the dancer's limbs as structure: *"spokes or limbs extending outward from
the center of the circle."*

**Under `HW-C8`'s clarifier this is recorded `frame-silent`, and that is the honest call** — saying
*"that is a prabhamandala, not a rose window"* requires an imported premise about two traditions,
which §2.1 forbids as grounds for `frame-contradicts`. A purely pictorial objection does exist (the
surround is **open at the bottom**, and its centre holds a dancer rather than a hub), but whether
that falsifies *"circular"* is arguable — and §6.3 says arguable stays `frame-silent`.

**This is the first live instance of the under-counting `HW-C8` §3 declared it was choosing.** The
decision predicted the cost in the abstract; here is the cost, concrete, on a claim that is
substantively wrong. **The rule held, and it should be re-examined with this case in hand** —
`HW-C8` §7.1 names exactly this as its overturn condition.
