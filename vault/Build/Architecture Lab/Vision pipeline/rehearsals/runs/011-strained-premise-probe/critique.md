# 011 — critique

*Fable-pass.*

## 1. The objection that most threatens this run: the deflationary reading

**"Immunisation" attributes arrangement to the model.** A flatter explanation is available:
LLM prose generates each section semi-independently, and two independently generated passages
ordinarily fail to interact. **Compatibility between the claim and the contradictions is exactly
what independence predicts.** On that reading the phenomenon does not exist as named — there is no
immunising *move*, only a failure of the two halves to meet.

**What speaks against it, and it is suggestive rather than decisive:** the concessions sit *inside*
the contradiction section rather than beside it. *"While both are intricate…"*, *"an interpretation
of the motif rather than the motif itself"*, *"the first image uses the **idea** … the second shows
the **actual**"* — the model re-attests the premise **at the moment of conceding**. Independent
generation would not reliably produce that.

**And the "or, more broadly" hedge arrives before any objection does**, which is harder to explain
as non-interaction: the escape hatch is built in the opening sentence.

**The cheapest test that would settle it is a verbatim repeat**, not a new arm. Recorded as the
run's first unresolved question and carried into the repeat-stability design.

## 2. The curator's prior, and where it could have distorted the reading

I ran this expecting spark-10 to replicate, and it did. That is the shape of confirmation bias, and
the run should be read with it in view.

**Two things partly protect it.** The decisive question was fixed in the manifest *before* the call,
as an explicit three-step procedure over the model's own formulation. And the pair's strain was
established by **HW-L4 and HW-C4, in earlier cycles, by lanes with no stake in this question** — I
could not have chosen a pair to suit the result without contradicting the program's own record.

**What is not protected:** the judgement in §1 of `score.md` that each contradiction is *compatible*
with the model's formulation. That is interpretive, and a reader who thought "medium and material"
genuinely bears on *"the rose window"* would score this differently. **I think they would be wrong —
a shadow of a rose window is still a rose window — but the argument is available and I have not
hidden it.**

## 3. What would have falsified this run

- **RESISTS** — the model narrowing the premise to superficiality or rejecting it — was
  pre-registered and would have **reversed the A6 decision to GO-WITH-NEW-INSTRUMENT** and revised
  010's framing in the model's favour. It was a live outcome; the pair is genuinely mismatched.
- **BALANCED** would have left A6 at NO-GO pending further work.
- A declined or anomalous response would have licensed nothing.

**The branch that would have saved A6 was on the board and pre-registered.**

## 4. The unplanned result is the more valuable one

This run was built to test spark-10. **It weakened spark-08 instead**, and that was nobody's
intention: the same prompt as arm E, on a different pair, named a monument where arm E named none.

*(Fable-pass.)* **This is the first time in the program that evidence has pushed back on a spark
rather than accumulating behind it.** Three runs had lined up behind "identity claims track the
question", and one changed fixture undid the clean version. It is worth noticing that the correction
arrived as a *side effect* — the program's designed probes have been good at confirming and this
one, aimed elsewhere, was the one that disconfirmed.

**The lesson to carry: vary the thing you are holding constant, occasionally, on purpose.** Four
runs held the pair fixed to isolate the question; that discipline was right, and it also hid a
factor for four runs.

## 5. A measurement correction nobody asked for

Five Pair-2 calls suggested a stable ~1794 tokens/image. **This call measured 3109 prompt tokens on
*larger* files.** So the constant was never per-image or per-byte — it tracks **pixel area / tile
count**, and looked stable only because one pair was held fixed across five runs.

**HB-008 and HB-009 both published the ~1794 figure as a program-wide budgeting constant. That was
wrong and is corrected here.** It cost nothing, because every run has been far under the ceiling,
but a future two-image run on large fixtures could have been mis-budgeted on it.

## 6. On retiring A6

Retiring an instrument after five cycles of gating deserves more discomfort than a tidy verdict
allows. **What is being given up is real:** A6 was the program's only designed test of
*suggestibility* — whether attributing a claim to a third party changes what the model will say —
and nothing replaces it. The ladder answers a neighbouring question well and that one not at all.

**The honest position:** the device is retired because its measurement is impossible against a
ceiling, **not** because the question was uninteresting. If a future run ever finds a premise the
model resists, the attribution device becomes measurable again immediately, and this retirement
should be revisited rather than cited as settled.
