## spark-09 — REVISED, and sharpened into two claims

Previously (008): *"relational vocabulary is an attractor — the noun is the variable."* **That
formulation is now too coarse and should be retired in favour of the following.**

**spark-09a — the noun licenses the register.** Asking for a *motif* moves the model into
purpose-and-atmosphere talk: *"intended to evoke a sense of the divine"*, *"symbolizing a connection
to heaven"*. Arm D produced this **per image, without any comparison**.

**spark-09b — the relational demand licenses the joint predication.** The move from *"this image
evokes the divine"* to *"**both images share** a fundamental motif of creating a sense of the
divine"* required being asked what the two share. **The content of the kinship was already
available; what the demand supplied was the act of predicating it jointly.**

**Evidence: a 2×2 with one filled corner, on identical bytes.**

| | no relational demand | relational demand |
|---|---|---|
| **without *motif*** | arm B — `KIN-ABSENT` | arm C — `KIN-SPECIFIC-ONLY`, denies sameness |
| **with *motif*** | **arm D — `KIN-ABSENT`** | arm A — `KIN-HEDGED`, asserts a shared motif |

**Neither ingredient is sufficient alone.** Strength: SPARK, n=1 pair, 1 model, 4 calls total across
runs 008 and 009.

**What would strengthen it:** a frequency-matched control (repeat *motif* twice without the
relational demand — see `critique.md` §3); a second pair; the same cross on other relational
vocabularies (*influence*, *lineage*, *belongs to the same tradition*).

**What would kill it:** arm D asserting kinship on a repeat sample, which would mean this run caught
a single low-temperature draw rather than a dissociation.

## spark-08 — CONTROLLED REPLICATION, on identical bytes

008 found identity-reaching claims concentrated in the arm asked *"say what it is"* — it named
**both** monuments; the motif and construction arms named none. **009 is a second motif-framed call
on the same bytes and it named no monument either.**

| arm | question | monuments named | identity-reaching ledger rows |
|---|---|---|---|
| B (008) | "say what it is" | **2** | 3 |
| A (008) | "what motif" | 0 | 2 |
| **D (009)** | **"what motif"** | **0** | **1** |

Three calls, one stimulus, byte-identical. **The pictures are held perfectly constant, so the
variance is attributable to the question alone.** This is now the cleanest evidence in the program
that *what the model imports tracks what it is asked, not what it is shown* — cleaner than the
cycle-6 audit, which compared across runs differing in fixture, budget and date.

**Still a SPARK.** One stimulus pair; the arms differ in more than question-type; and in both runs
this was a side-observation of a run designed for something else.
