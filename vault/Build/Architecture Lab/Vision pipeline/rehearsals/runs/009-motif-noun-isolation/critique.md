# 009 — critique

*Fable-pass.*

## 1. The result is a clean dissociation, and that is rarer than it sounds

Four calls, one stimulus, byte-identical throughout. Two ingredients — the noun *motif*, and a
relational demand — crossed against each other:

| | no relational demand | relational demand |
|---|---|---|
| **without *motif*** | arm B: `KIN-ABSENT` | arm C: `KIN-SPECIFIC-ONLY` (denies sameness) |
| **with *motif*** | **arm D: `KIN-ABSENT`** | arm A: `KIN-HEDGED` (asserts a shared motif) |

**Only the conjunction cell produced kinship.** That is a 2×2 with one filled corner, assembled
across two runs, on one image pair. It is the strongest shape this program has produced, and it
should still be read as n=1 on the pair.

## 2. The honest exposure: this reading was not blind, and it confirmed my expectation

008's arms were scored before 009 ran. The model is unaffected — every call is stateless — but
**I am not a blind reader**, and `KIN-ABSENT` is the outcome that makes the 2×2 tidy.

What protects the reading is that the datum is **an absence of a whole section**, not a judgement
about tone. Arm D either contains a cross-image sentence or it does not; it does not, and that is
checkable by anyone in ten seconds against the frozen text. Had the deciding evidence been another
hedge to adjudicate — as arm A's was — the non-blindness would be a serious problem. Here it is a
declared weakness rather than a live one.

**What would remove the doubt:** a pre-registered arm D run *before* arm A was scored. That
ordering is no longer available and cannot be recovered by argument.

## 3. The confound I introduced by deleting the sentence

Deleting the relational clause also deleted the **second occurrence of the word *motif***. Arm A
says it twice ("what motif it carries", "features of these motifs"); arm D says it once.

So arm D is not a pure removal of the presupposition — it is *presupposition removed **and** noun
salience halved*. A sceptic can say arm D fell silent because the noun was under-primed, not because
the presupposition was doing the work.

**I do not think that is the right reading**, because arm B (which contains no *motif* at all) and
arm D (one *motif*) produced the **same** outcome, while arm A (two) and arm C (zero *motif* but a
relational demand) diverge on the relational axis rather than the noun-count axis. The variance
tracks the demand, not the frequency. **But that is an argument, not a measurement**, and the
measurement is cheap: repeat the noun without the demand (*"say what motif image 1 carries, and what
motif image 2 carries"*). One call. Declared and not run.

## 4. What would have falsified this run

- **Arm D asserting or hedging a shared motif** would have given the other principal cell — the noun
  carries it — and would have meant deleting the sameness assertion from A6's analogy could never
  disarm it. That was a live possibility: arm D had both images in one call, and co-presence alone
  is a standing invitation to compare.
- **Arm D declining to name motifs at all** would have been anomalous and would have licensed
  nothing.

The run could have come out otherwise, and the losing branch was pre-registered in the manifest
before the call.

## 5. Where this leaves A6 — a caution against reading it as clearance

009 makes cycle 7's re-derived delta *better anchored*, and it is tempting to read that as A6 being
more ready than it was. **It is not.** 009 also exposed that the delta (arm A → stage 2) still moves
**two** things: demand→assertion and no-attribution→attribution. That residual was invisible before
this run, because before this run "the vocabulary" was one undifferentiated lump.

**Each cycle that examines A6 finds one more variable inside it.** Cycle 6 found the kinship
vocabulary was untested. Cycle 7 found the delta needed re-deriving. Cycle 8 finds the sameness
*move* is separable from the sameness *word*. That is either a program converging on a well-specified
instrument, or a sign that A6's two-stage device bundles too much to be measured by subtraction at
all.

*(Fable-pass, and I think this is the thing to decide next cycle.)* **The honest question is no
longer "is A6 ready" but "is A6 the right instrument".** A6 asks whether a third party's analogy can
make the model claim kinship. Three single calls have now shown the model's kinship behaviour is
controlled by question shape at a granularity finer than A6's design contemplates. An instrument
whose signal is smaller than the confounds surrounding it is worth redesigning, not gating harder.
**Arm E — assert sameness without attribution — is one call and would settle it.**

## 6. Two smaller things worth keeping

- **The model disambiguated an idiom the curator had to adjudicate.** 008 §4 judged *"draws the eye
  upward"* as the viewer's eye, not anthropomorphism. Arm D wrote *"drawing the **viewer's** eye"*.
  The model made the same distinction unprompted. Weak evidence, but it is evidence, and it lands on
  the one contestable call in 008.
- **The "sense of the divine" phrase appears in both arm A and arm D**, applied to two images jointly
  in one and to one image alone in the other. The *content* was always available; only the
  **joint predication** was frame-dependent. This is the run's most quotable result and it belongs in
  spark-09's next revision.
