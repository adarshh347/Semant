# Foundry Critique — FS-002-textual-dynamism

**Sandbox:** FS-002-textual-dynamism · **Axis:** textual-dynamism · **RESEARCH-ONLY**

> The critique argues against the run's own score. A cycle with no correction is either genuinely
> clean or insufficiently adversarial — and this document must say which.

---

## 1. The strongest case against the result — **raised, tested, and REFUTED within this run**

The objection I raised first: **`source_condition` may not mean what the score assumes.** The schema
only enumerates `present | absent | replaced | misleading` without defining the term, so an equally
consistent reading was available — that the field marks *"no adversarial source manipulation
applied"* rather than *"a source text was supplied"*. Under that reading the eleven manifests are not
false, and R3 collapses.

**I then ran the check §5 demanded, and the alternative reading is dead.**
`R1/R1-schema-decisions.md` defines the field verbatim:

> `source_condition` (present|absent|replaced|misleading) — so a run records **whether the source was
> there, removed, swapped, or misleading** (source-ventriloquism / sequence-confounding guards).

`present` means *the source was there*. It was not. **R3 stands, and is now established rather than
contested.** The score's reading was correct and I could not break it.

**But the same search produced a correction that matters more** — see §4.

## 2. Deflationary reading

**Nothing was discovered; a field was over-read and a known result was restated.**

Strip the run down and it says: (a) prompts change model output — already established as spark-06
and spark-09, not by this sandbox; (b) no instrumented run had a source text — visible from any
manifest at a glance; (c) three thinkers are not mentioned in the vault — a `grep`.

The genuinely new content is the **conjunction** — that the program's safeguard against source
authority has never been under load — and even that is an argument from absence. A deflationary
reader would say FS-002 performed bookkeeping and wrote it up with the vocabulary of discovery.

**This reading is strong.** The score's defence is R4 (ventriloquism untested rather than defeated),
which is a real reframe with a real consequence. But a reframe is cheaper than a finding, and this
critique declines to call it more than that.

## 3. Confounds

- **Two modes conflated in one table.** E1 lists imaginative and instrumented runs together and then
  draws a conclusion about instrumented ones. The imaginative rows are the only truthful `present`
  values, and their presence in the table makes the contradiction look larger than it is.
- **spark-06 and spark-09 are not this run's evidence.** They were produced by runs 007–009 for other
  questions. FS-002 borrowed them. Any strength they lend is inherited, not earned here.
- **Non-blind analyst.** I wrote FS-002's manifest — including the claim that the human-prose control
  was "deliberate" — then scored against my own rubric. Notably **the human-prose control
  (`vault/Writing/Stories and motives/plan.md`) was frozen as a seed and never actually used** in
  the scoring. It was designed in and then quietly dropped.
- **`grep` as evidence of absence.** E5 concludes Casey/Warburg/Didi-Huberman are absent from a
  name-match over `.md` files. A thinker can pressure a run without being named.

## 4. Method corrections

**Correction 1 — the human-prose control was declared and not used.** FS-002's manifest names a
control whose whole purpose was to prevent the measure separating nothing (its `notes` say exactly
this). The score never scores it. **The rubric's `discrimination` row is therefore weaker than
`partially` — it was not tested against the one case designed to test it.** This is a genuine method
failure, not a presentational one.

**Correction 2 — the score's R4 is wrong about *why* sources are absent, and the plan says so
explicitly.** The score reads the absence of source texts as an untested gap. `R2-batch-operating-plan.md`
shows it was in part a **deliberate guard**: run A3's lens is recorded as
*"choreography — **not** Merleau-Ponty; avoid source ventriloquism"*. The program did not forget to
supply sources; in at least one designed run it **removed a source on purpose to avoid the very
failure mode R4 says is untested.**

R4 must be restated: ventriloquism is untested **because the program designed it out**, which is a
defensible methodological choice — not, as the score implies, an oversight. The consequence stands
(the safeguard has never been under load) but the tone was unearned.

**Correction 3 — and this is the cycle's real finding.** The same plan shows **A6 was the only run
ever designed with `source_condition: misleading`** — the single adversarial source-manipulation run
in the entire program. **A6 was RETIRED** (HB-010 §3). So the program retired its only designed test
of source pressure, for reasons that had nothing to do with sources (the agreement ceiling made its
*attribution* measurement impossible). **A capability was lost as collateral damage of an unrelated
retirement, and no document notices.** HB-010 §3 does record that A6 was "the program's only designed
test of suggestibility" and that "nothing replaces it" — but frames that as being about
suggestibility, not about source condition.

**Does an earlier conclusion fall?** No. spark-06 and spark-09 are untouched — this run neither
strengthens nor weakens them, and must not be cited as replication of either. A6's retirement is
**not** reopened here: the ceiling argument that retired it is independent of this observation.

## 5. What would kill this finding

- ~~**A definition of `source_condition`** matching §1's alternative reading — would kill R3~~
  **SEARCHED AND SETTLED.** `R1-schema-decisions.md` defines it as *"whether the source was there"*.
  R3 survives its own strongest objection.
- **One instrumented run with a source slice**, A/B'd against `absent`. If the source changes
  nothing, R1's "unknown" resolves to "sources are inert here" and the whole axis narrows sharply.
  If it changes something, R2's "untested safeguard" becomes urgent rather than theoretical.
- **Any run where a philosopher's vocabulary appears in model output without appearing in the
  prompt.** That would show ventriloquism is possible after all, and refute R4.

## 7. Recorded discomfort

R5 ("the flame-stimulator form already exists and works") is the claim I most want to be true and
can least support. `source_pressures` with `paraphrase` + `locator` is elegant, and it has been used
in exactly **one** run, in imaginative mode, by an author who was not under pressure to cite anything.
Calling it "works" is generous; it has not been stressed.

Second discomfort: the Warburg observation in the score's O2 is the most interesting sentence in this
run and is pure speculation. It arrived because I had just written FS-001 and was carrying its
vocabulary. That is precisely the sequence-confounding the doctrine warns about — an insight
attributed to the material that was manufactured by the order of work.
