## 3. Synthesis of the three findings — how exposed is A6 really?

The two findings are usually described as A3 over-claiming and A4 correcting it. **That description
is too generous to A4, and A6 must not be designed on it.**

**What A3 shows.** Given `695be843` — a dark metal structure, no figure — under an *address-framed*
question, the model produced a face, eyes, a gaze, an intention, and then reasoned confidently from
the whole invented structure to a comparative judgement it got right by an unanticipated route. The
invention was not hedged. It was premise.

**What A4 shows.** Given `695be784` — a frontal three-arched aniconic wall with a lit central opening
— under *organisation-framed* questions that never said face/eye/gaze/body, no face reading appeared.

**Why this is not yet a correction.** A4 changed **two variables at once**: the question frame *and*
the stimulus. The spark-06 correction attributes the whole difference to the frame. But there is a
live third hypothesis the record does not address:

> **The eye reading may require a *pair* of apertures.** A3's structure is a facade with rows of
> windows — plural, lateral, paired. A4's wall has **one** lit opening, on the centre axis. A single
> central aperture is not an eye configuration; it is a mouth or a niche. On the pareidolia
> hypothesis, A4's image is not "the most face-suggestive image in the corpus" at all — it is
> frontal and symmetric but **eyeless by construction**, and it would have produced no face under
> *either* prompt.

So the honest state is: **neither "spontaneous" nor "question-conditional" is established.** A3 is a
positive result with a confounded prompt; A4 is a negative result with a confounded stimulus. A
negative result from a stimulus that may lack the triggering feature bounds nothing. `CANDIDATE-REGISTER.md`
and `005/sparks.md` both already state the caveat ("this rests on a negative result and the settling
A/B was not run") — this gate's position is that the caveat is **stronger than the correction**, and
spark-06 should be recorded as **UNRESOLVED (two competing narrowings)** rather than as narrowed to
question-conditional.

**Stated position on A6's exposure.**

- **Stage-1 anthropomorphism exposure: MODERATE-TO-HIGH, and not reduced by A4.** `695be8ec`'s
  muqarnas hood is hundreds of small dark cell-cavities in bilateral symmetry about a vertical axis —
  the *paired-aperture* condition in its most extreme available form, far more so than A3's windows.
  If the pairing hypothesis is right, this is the strongest pareidolia stimulus in the corpus. A4's
  no-face result gives no protection here, because A4's image lacked the very feature at issue.
  `695be815`'s boss-and-rings is iris-shaped but singular — lower exposure, opposite geometry.
- **Stage-2 hedging exposure: HIGH, and it is the more likely spoiler.** Across A2, A3 and A4 this
  model has never once refused, never once stalled, and has volunteered unrequested cultural
  attribution when explicitly forbidden. L4's prediction that HEDGED is the modal outcome is
  well-supported by the batch's own record.
- **The two interact, and that is the real risk.** If both crops are anthropomorphised in stage 1,
  the analogy stage 2 offers becomes "two gazes upward" — the easiest kinship available — and the
  D2 delta stops measuring analogy acceptance at all. This is a *forecastable* contamination, so it
  must be pre-committed as a reinterpretation rule (§5), not adjudicated afterwards.

**Residual uncertainty, stated exactly:** whether the projection disposition is elicited by the
*question frame*, by *paired apertures in the stimulus*, or by both — and therefore whether A6's
stage 1 is safe under a neutral prompt on a highly pareidolic crop.

**The single cheap test that resolves it — the A/B that spark-06 already names, run on the RIGHT
image.** Take `695be843` — the image that *already produced the face* — and put two fresh, stateless
prompts to it: one address-framed (A3's), one structure-framed (A4's). One image, native resolution,
two calls, ~2100 tokens each, well inside budget; the same shape as A4, which ran clean.

- Face survives the structure-framed prompt ⇒ **A3's spontaneity claim stands**, the spark-06
  correction is wrong, and A6's anthropomorphism cell must be promoted to a primary outcome with its
  scoring rewritten before A6 runs.
- Face disappears ⇒ **question-conditionality is confirmed on a fixed stimulus, with the image
  confound removed**, spark-06's correction is earned, and A6's stage 1 is credible as a baseline.

Either way it is decided in two cheap calls instead of being argued across three cycles. Because
`695be843` is a **deliberate re-run of a spent image**, it must be declared as replication in
`source_notes` and its result must **not** be counted as a new independent corpus observation.
