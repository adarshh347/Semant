# VISION-E · E4 — Retrieval evaluation before product claims — report

**Date:** 2026-07-19 · **Branch:** `feat/vision-e-embeddings`.

## The evaluated corpus

All **9 dissected posts** in the connected DB were embedded (93 DINOv2 vectors across
identity/context/whole + 4 FashionCLIP garment vectors). Real coverage:

- **figurative / sculpture** — a 10-region set of dynastic heads (Solanki / Gupta / Pala faces
  and crowns) + several single-person figures;
- **architecture** — walls/floors (the Pietà + a 7-region interior);
- **painting** — a 16-region portrait (hair/face/top/scarf) + two single-figure paintings;
- **fashion** — 4 garment regions (top/scarf/dress/jacket), all from **one** painting.

This is a small, art-heavy, single-curator corpus — the evaluation is honest about what that
does and does not let us conclude.

## Results (top-5, DINOv2 mask-pooled `visual_identity`, FashionCLIP `fashion`)

| space | queries | same-label@1 | same-label@5 | cross-post@1 |
|-------|---------|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| visual_identity | 38 | 21% | **55%** | 4/38 |
| visual_context  | 38 | 18% | 53% | 4/38 |
| fashion | 4 | — (all one image) | — | — |

**Useful within-domain neighbours (successes):** the correct label is in the top-5 over **half**
the time; clear hits include `face → face 0.88`, `crown → Gupta crown 0.76`, and the fashion
`top → jacket 0.99`. Montages in `E4-evidence/` (`face_query`, `wall_query`, `crown_query`) show
the query identity crop beside its top-3 neighbours.

## Failure categories (recorded from the data)

1. **Material / colour dominance** — the single biggest effect. DINOv2 groups by the stone /
   terracotta / marble surface and lighting, so faces, crowns and torsos of *one* sculpture score
   0.79–0.85 against each other regardless of what they depict (`crown → face 0.85`). Colour and
   material, not the object, drive rank-1.
2. **Same-image / spatial contamination** — cross-post@1 is only **4/38**: the nearest neighbour
   is almost always an *adjacent part of the same artwork*. Mask pooling shares one patch grid, so
   neighbouring parts bleed into each other; context crops share the whole scene (`wall → person
   0.86` in context space).
3. **wall ↔ floor / structure confusion** — architectural stone surfaces are near-identical to the
   backbone (`wall → floor 0.59`); structure is under-discriminated.
4. **Thin, skewed corpus** — fashion is 4 vectors from one image (retrieval is trivial/self-
   referential); architecture and true cross-artwork fashion are under-represented. No conclusion
   about cross-corpus fashion retrieval is warranted yet.

## Per-space defaults (chosen only from this evidence)

- **`visual_identity` is the default visual space**, but rank-1 is noisy (material-dominated), so
  retrieval is **top-k research assistance, never an assertion** — the curator judges (exactly the
  E5 contract). Neighbours are never auto-promoted to Motifs/Relations.
- **Offer an "other artworks only" (exclude-same-post) toggle** — same-post neighbours are trivial
  adjacency; cross-artwork is where the signal (and the failure) actually lives.
- **`visual_context` is secondary** — it adds scene contamination without beating identity here;
  offer it for "in-situ" queries, don't default to it.
- **No universal/fused ranking.** Scores across spaces are not comparable (E3), and this evidence
  gives no calibrated fusion — so none is built.

## OpenCLIP decision (the E3 deferral, now evidence-based)

**Keep OpenCLIP deferred.** DINOv2 visual retrieval is material-dominated and same-image-biased,
which text↔evidence search *might* cut through — but on this thin, single-domain corpus there is
no way to demonstrate OpenCLIP earns its place over DINOv2, which the contract requires before
adding it. Revisit when a real cross-domain corpus exists (Increment F backfill provides it).

**Gate result: PASS** — reviewed examples demonstrate useful within-domain neighbours
(same-label@5 = 55%, concrete face/crown/garment hits) and the weak queries are honestly
documented (material dominance, same-image contamination, thin fashion/architecture coverage).
