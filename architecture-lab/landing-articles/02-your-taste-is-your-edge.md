# Your taste is your edge

The feed is full of the same face now. The same AI-smoothed light, the same borrowed caption, the same generated thumbnail everyone has already learned to scroll past. When everyone can generate everything, the one thing that can't be faked is a point of view. Your taste — the specific way *you* see — is the last unautomatable thing you own.

The problem is that most people can't say what their taste *is*. They feel it. They know a good image when it stops them. But ask them why, part by part, and it goes quiet — which means it can't be repeated, can't be defended to a client, can't be turned into a caption that sounds like a person instead of a bot.

Darshan is a tool for making your eye articulate.

You bring it the images that stop you — from your feed, your work, anywhere. It reads them with you, part by part, and lets you say what each one does to you. Over time it learns the shape of your attention: what you keep returning to, the details you always catch, the tension you're drawn to. Not a mood board — a memory of how you see.

And then it writes *with* you, not *for* you. When you write a caption, a lookbook line, a note — the actual visual detail is right there, grounded in the real image and in your own accumulated voice. No generic slop. No "✨ obsessed with this vibe ✨." Words that sound like the person who saw the thing.

Anyone can generate an image. Almost no one can tell you why theirs is good. That gap is your edge. This is where you sharpen it.
