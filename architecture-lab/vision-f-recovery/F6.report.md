# VISION-F · F6 — Retrieval quality after recovery — report

**Date:** 2026-07-20 · **Branch:** `feat/vision-f-recovery`.

## What changed since the E baseline

No new posts were dissected (the corpus did not grow); instead F3 **replaced box-legacy crops with
authoritative masks** on the recovered posts, so their identity vectors are now **mask-pooled**
(evidence-isolated) rather than bbox-crop CLS. F6 re-runs the E4 evaluation on the refreshed
embeddings to measure the effect.

## Results vs E baseline

| metric | E4 baseline (box/crop) | F6 (after recovery) | Δ |
|--------|------------------------|---------------------|---|
| identity same-label@1 | 21% (8/38) | 24% (9/38) | +3 pt |
| identity **same-label@5** | 55% (21/38) | **68% (26/38)** | **+13 pt** |
| identity cross-post@1 | 4/38 | 4/38 | 0 |
| context same-label@1 | 18% | 32% | +14 pt |
| context same-label@5 | 53% | 63% | +10 pt |

Routes now: identity **37 `mask_pool` + 1 `crop_cls`** (was all `crop_cls`/box-legacy) — the
recovery is what moved the numbers.

## Reading the change

- **What improved:** within-domain usefulness. Identity same-label@**5** rose **55% → 68%** —
  mask pooling isolates the evidence from its background, so a face retrieves faces (and a garment
  garments) more often in the top-5. Context retrieval also improved (tighter mask bbox framing).
- **What did not:** **same-image bias** — cross-post@1 stayed 4/38. The nearest neighbour is still
  usually an adjacent part of the same artwork; that is a **corpus-density** problem (9 dissected
  posts), not a geometry one, and recovery cannot fix it.
- **Material / colour dominance** still caps rank-1 on the single-material sculpture set (@1 only
  24%), though it is less severe than with box crops.
- **identity vs context:** context edges identity at @1 (32% vs 24%) but identity wins at @5
  (68% vs 63%); both are offered — identity remains the default (E4 decision holds).

## OpenCLIP / fusion decision (F6)

**Still deferred.** The improvement came from better *geometry*, not from a new model, and the
corpus did not grow — so there is no new evidence that text↔image (OpenCLIP) or score fusion would
beat DINOv2. Adding OpenCLIP "because retrieval matters more now" is exactly the move the contract
warns against. Re-evaluate only when a genuinely larger, multi-domain corpus exists (post-F).

**Gate result: PASS** — the comparison against the E baseline shows a clear, measured improvement
from recovery (identity @5 +13 pt), honestly reports what did not change (same-image bias, corpus
density), and concludes retrieval is **ready for ordinary within-domain research use** — as
assistance, not fact — with no new model warranted.
