# 10 orientation targets — use-case & marketing (July 2026 research)

## The recommended orientation (read first)
Lead **B2C** with a sharp end, monetize **B2B** with the aggregate. The sharpest, most defensible, social-native end:

> **Help visual creators (fashion-first) understand and articulate their own taste — their aesthetic "edge" — and write authentic, image-grounded content from it, instead of drowning in generic AI slop and moodboard drift.**

This threads four confirmed market facts: (1) in the creator economy "being authentically *you* is your edge," and **AI slop is visibly losing** its click edge as audiences learn to spot it; (2) the curation tools people already love (Cosmos, Are.na, Savee) **save and organize but never articulate or write** — that's the open gap; (3) fashion is where "why this works, part by part" is a daily paid job and the pipeline is deepest; (4) you have the **social connection** (extension reads the feed, taste accrues). The B2B side (aggregate part-level taste → brands, Track F) is the later revenue, and it's a *finer* signal than the watch-time/attribute-counting incumbents sell.

## The competitive gap (the wedge, in one line)
Cosmos auto-tags colour/mood/style; Are.na is a research journal; Savee is editorial bookmarking; WGSN/Heuritech forecast at market scale. **All of them stop at "what is it / what's trending." None reads *why it moves you, part by part,* learns your taste as language, and writes from it.** That triad — felt-meaning + taste-as-language + grounded generation — is unoccupied.

## The 10 targets (ranked by fit)
| # | Target | The end / pain to solve | Darshan's answer | Fit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Fashion creatives / stylists / creative directors** | Articulate *why* a look works, part by part; moodboard drift; storytelling is tacit | Part-level felt reading + grounded editorial/lookbook copy | ★★★ lead |
| 2 | **Visual content creators building an aesthetic** | Stay consistent with their "edge"; write authentic captions, not AI slop | Read their own feed's taste; write image-grounded, on-voice copy | ★★★ lead |
| 3 | **Aesthetic curators / visual researchers (Cosmos/Are.na/Savee users)** | Save endlessly but never *think* — boards are archives, not decisions | The close-reading + writing layer those tools lack ("Are.na that reads & writes with you") | ★★★ adjacent-direct |
| 4 | **Brand social / content marketers** | On-brand, consistent, non-slop visual content + copy | Learn the brand's visual signature → generate grounded, on-aesthetic content | ★★ |
| 5 | **Cultural / trend analysts & insight agencies (B2B)** | Finer cultural signal than attribute-counting / watch-time | Aggregate part-level felt-taste signals (Track F chain) | ★★ (monetization) |
| 6 | **Advertising / creative agencies** | Turn scattered references into a coherent *brief*, fast | Moodboard-with-reasoning → a real brief, not a drifting board | ★★ |
| 7 | **Architecture / interior designers** | Read elements, materials, light, spatial mood | Same machine, architectural lenses (2nd vertical) | ★★ |
| 8 | **Photographers / photo editors** | Cull + articulate composition/light with reasoning | Close-read the frame; reasoned selection notes | ★ |
| 9 | **Art & design educators / students / critics** | Teach/practice visual close-reading; storytelling as originality | The "intellectualising through media" tool — reading as pedagogy | ★ (resonance > size) |
| 10 | **Visual essayists / cultural critics / writers** | Write *grounded* in images, not detached from them | The "third thing": resurfacing + /part + /lens grounded writing | ★ (purest, niche) |

Honourable mention (the wedge user, not a market): **the personal visual thinker** — your own honest, nerdy use case. It's the seed, not the pitch.

## Why not the crowded lanes
Don't try to out-tag Ximilar/Cosmos (auto-tagging is commoditized) or out-forecast WGSN/Heuritech (6,500 brands, data-science moats). Both are *reductive* to a label or number and enterprise-shaped. Darshan is *generative* of meaning and prose, and creator-shaped. Compete on the axis nobody owns.

## The two-sided model (already locked, now market-grounded)
- **B2C wedge:** creators/curators articulate taste + write from it (targets 1–3). Low-friction, social-native, anti-slop.
- **B2B revenue:** the aggregate of *what parts move people* becomes cultural-taste intelligence brands pay for (target 5, Track F) — finer than watch-time (which is confounded by duration) or attribute counts.
